Ph.D. Research Title

“The efficacy of application of ozonated water along with indicated Homoeopathic medicine in Psoriasis Vulgaris using the PASI score: A Randomized Control Trial.”

⸻

Research Area

The research focuses on the evaluation of integrated application of individualized Homoeopathic medicine along with ozonated water in the management of Psoriasis Vulgaris, assessed through the Psoriasis Area and Severity Index (PASI) score.

Significance of the Study

Psoriasis Vulgaris is a chronic inflammatory skin disorder that often affects physical comfort, appearance, and quality of life. This study is significant because it explores a structured and evidence-based approach to Homoeopathic management, combined with ozonated water, in a clinically measurable manner. The use of PASI score strengthens the scientific assessment of treatment response.

Expected Outcomes

The study is expected to assess whether the combined intervention produces measurable improvement in clinical severity, symptom relief, and overall patient well-being. It may also help identify the usefulness of individualized Homoeopathic prescription in conjunction with ozonated water as a supportive therapeutic approach.

Contribution of the Research

This research aims to contribute to the academic and clinical understanding of Homoeopathic treatment in dermatological conditions. It may provide useful evidence for future clinical studies, support repertorial and individualized case-based practice, and add to the growing body of research in integrative and outcome-oriented Homoeopathy.

Methodological Focus

* Clinical research in Homoeopathy
* Evaluation through validated clinical scoring system (PASI score)
* Repertorial analysis and individualized prescription
* Assessment of therapeutic outcomes
